SUCCESS STORY
Growing up, I always dreamed of making my mark in the world of Saudi
fashion. My journey began after I finished my studies in Clothing and
Textiles, when I took the leap and started my own workshop in 2019, with a
loan from Riyadah Program.
But just when things were starting to take off, the COVID-19 pandemic hit,
bringing unforeseen challenges. I found myself drowning in debt and the
operational costs just kept growing, weighing down on the profit margin.
But I refused to give up. Instead, I decided to face the challenge with more
hard work, focusing on manufacturing abayas. That decision changed
everything. I discovered new capabilities that made me more resilient.
Then came the NEOM Seven Senses Program, a game-changer for my
business. It helped me regain control, refine my strategy and take my
brand to new heights.
Thanks to the program, I got the chance to showcase my work at the
NEOM Seven Senses Boulevard and landed a major order. Now, with my
business thriving, I’m dreaming even bigger. I envision opening a larger
clothing factory in Tabuk, specializing in abayas and uniforms.
With NEOM Seven Senses, ambition knows no bounds!

The NEOM Culinary Arts Program by NEOM Hotel Division provides opportunities for
students from the NEOM and Tabuk communities to become professional chefs within
NEOM projects. Through a collaboration with ZADK Academy, the two-year program
offers students a specialized international diploma in culinary arts. Launched in 2022,
the program blends culinary and pastry skills with crucial professional tools like English
Language and Communication. Internships are vital for the program, with at least
450 hours required during the year.
During 2023, nine NEOM-sponsored students from the program’s first batch
completed their required internship abroad, a first for ZADK Academy. Recognizing
the immense value of international exposure for cultivating local talent, the NEOM
Hotel Division team chose Marriott as their inaugural partner for this groundbreaking
experience. The goal is to equip students with the necessary skills required for
employment at the Marriott Hotel on Sindalah Island upon their graduation.
Students have the opportunity to familiarize themselves with the general professional
environment and Marriott’s unique work culture.
The students’ results were remarkable, with some surpassing the required hours,
including Nouf Mansour (686 hours), Rakan Alsulami (673 hours) and Alrooh Alrweli
(471 hours), showcasing exceptional dedication and promise.
Aligning with NEOM’s future hospitality landscape, particularly in the upcoming island
destination, Sindalah, the program promises to unlock further opportunities for local
talent to contribute directly to the future of tourism in NEOM.

NATURE RESERVE
R A N G E R S
NEOM Rangers are the frontline personnel responsible for
monitoring and protecting the natural resources and wildlife within
NEOM. They enforce regulations related to conservation, including
preventing poaching, habitat destruction and other illegal activities.
In addition, they collect data, conduct patrols and report on the
status of biodiversity and habitat to support the goals of these
plans. Rangers also engage with local communities and educate
them about conservation efforts.
In 2023, Rangers program Phase 1 was launched, and a total of
38 rangers were enrolled in an advanced six-month field training
camp which was designed, developed, and delivered by South Africa
Wildlife College (SAWC). The training camp aims to develop the
rangers’ skills in protecting the reserves on land and underwater,
such as surviving the outdoors, patrolling, communication,
inspection, reporting and law enforcement.
