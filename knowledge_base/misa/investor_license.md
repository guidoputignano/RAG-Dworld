# Foreign Investor Licensing (MISA)

The Ministry of Investment of Saudi Arabia (MISA) is the primary channel through
which foreign investors and foreign-national founders register to operate in the
Kingdom. Under the 2024 Investment Law, foreign investors may hold up to 100%
ownership in most activities, subject to the activity code and sector.

## Who registers with MISA

- Foreign nationals establishing a wholly foreign-owned entity.
- Foreign investors taking equity in Saudi companies.
- Regional or strategic investors planning a multi-country mandate (see the RHQ
  Programme).

Saudi-national founders typically register through the national portal
(my.gov.sa) rather than MISA.

## What the licence enables

- Legal foreign ownership of the registered activity.
- Access to MISA e-services for amendments, property, and integrations with
  ZATCA (tax) and Absher (resident services).
- A pathway toward investor-category Premium Residency for qualifying investors.

## Next step

Register your interest at https://investsaudi.sa/en and download the MISA
Investor Guide (PDF-01) at
https://misa.gov.sa/app/uploads/2025/03/Investor-Guide.pdf. Individual structure,
